





1st Session, 37th Parliament,
49-50 Elizabeth II, 2001 










House of Commons of Canada










BILL C-273









An Act to amend the Parliament of Canada 
Act (recognized political parties)







R.S., c. P-1


Her Majesty, by and with the advice and 
consent of the Senate and House of Commons 
of Canada, enacts as follows:






R.S., c. 42 (1st 
Supp.), s. 2; 
1991, c. 20, s. 
2; 1997, c. 32, 
s. 1


1. Section 50 of the Parliament of Canada 
Act is amended by adding the following 
after subsection (1):







Recognized 
political 
parties


(1.1) The following definition applies in 
subsection (2):







``recognized 
political 
party''
« parti 
reconnu »


``recognized political party'' means a party 
that has a recognized membership in the 
House of Commons that










(a) is equal to or exceeds ten percent of the 
total membership of the House, and









(b) has members representing electoral 
districts in at least three provinces or 
territories.  






R.S., c. 42 (1st 
Supp.), s. 2; 
1991, c. 20, s. 
2; 1997, c. 32, 
s. 1


2. Subparagraphs 50(2)(b)(i) and (ii) of 
the Act are replaced with the following:










(i) the caucus of the party in opposition 
having the largest recognized 
membership in the House may appoint 
one member of the Board, and









(ii) the caucus of the party in opposition 
having the second largest recognized 
membership in the House may appoint 
one member of the Board if that party is 
a recognized political party, and if it is 
not, the caucus of the party mentioned in 
subparagraph (i) may appoint a second 
member of the Board.

3. Section 62 of the Act is renumbered as

Recognized

(1) The following definition applies in this

``recognized

``recognized political party'' means a party 
that has a recognized membership in the 
House of Commons that










(a) is equal to or exceeds ten percent of the

(b) has members representing electoral 
districts in at least three provinces or

1998, c. 23, s.

4. Paragraphs 62(2) of the Act are 
amended by striking out the expression 
``party that has a recognized membership of 
twelve or more persons in the House'' where 
it occurs in paragraphs (b), (d) and (f) and 
substituting in each case, the expression 
``recognized political party''.
