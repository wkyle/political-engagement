





1st Session, 37th Parliament,
49-50 Elizabeth II, 2001 










House of Commons of Canada










BILL C-257









An Act to amend the Criminal Code 
(attempting to disarm a peace officer)







R.S., 1985, 
c. C-46


Her Majesty, by and with the advice and 
consent of the Senate and House of Commons 
of Canada, enacts as follows:









1. The Criminal Code is amended by 
adding the following after section 270:







Disarming a 
peace officer


270.1 Every one who










(a) disarms or attempts to disarm a peace 
officer engaged in the execution of duty, or









(b) wilfully interferes with protective 
equipment issued to a peace officer,









is guilty of an indictable offence and liable to 
imprisonment for a term not exceeding five 
years.  







R.S., c. 27 
(1st Supp.); 
1992, c. 1; 
1994, c. 44; 
1995, c. 22; 
1996, c. 19; 
1997, c. 18; 
1999, c. 3


2. Paragraph 553(c) of the Act is 
amended by adding the following after 
subparagraph (vii):










(vii.1) section 270.1 (disarming a peace 
officer).
